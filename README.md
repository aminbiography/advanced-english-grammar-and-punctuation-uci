# Advanced Grammar & Punctuation Reference System

A comprehensive structured reference documenting English tense systems, clause structures, conjunctions, punctuation, and advanced grammar patterns. This document integrates rule-based explanations with professional sentence structures and applied examples.

## Part 1: The 12 Affirmative Tense Structures

| Tense | Core Function | Structure | Professional Example |
|------|-------------|----------|----------------------|
| Simple Past | Completed action in the past | Subject + past verb | I documented the results yesterday. |
| Past Progressive | Action in progress in past | Subject + was/were + V-ing | We were updating the system at noon. |
| Past Perfect | Completed before another past action | Subject + had + V3 | They had finished the report before the deadline. |
| Past Perfect Progressive | Duration before past point | Subject + had been + V-ing | I had been waiting for two hours before the call started. |
| Simple Present | Facts and routines | Subject + base verb | The program runs automatically. |
| Present Progressive | Action happening now | Subject + am/is/are + V-ing | I am working on the proposal now. |
| Present Perfect | Past with present relevance | Subject + have/has + V3 | We have launched the new feature. |
| Present Perfect Progressive | Duration up to now | Subject + have/has been + V-ing | She has been leading the team since 2024. |
| Simple Future | Decisions and predictions | Subject + will + base verb | I will send the invoice tomorrow. |
| Future (going to) | Planned actions | Subject + going to + verb | We are going to deploy the update tonight. |
| Future Progressive | Ongoing future action | Subject + will be + V-ing | He will be traveling all day Tuesday. |
| Future Perfect | Completed before future time | Subject + will have + V3 | I will have completed the tasks by Friday. |

## Negatives and Interrogatives

### Negatives
Rule:
```

Subject + Helping Verb + not + Main Verb

```

Example:
- They are working → They are not working

### Interrogatives
Rule:
```

Helping Verb + Subject + Main Verb?

```

Example:
- You have finished → Have you finished?

## Part 4: Stative Verbs (No Progressive Rule)

Stative verbs describe conditions, not actions.

| Category | Example | Correct Usage |
|---------|--------|--------------|
| Mental | know, believe | I understand |
| Emotion | love, want | I want coffee |
| Possession | have, own | I have a car |
| Senses | see, hear | You seem tired |
| Measurement | cost, weigh | It costs $10 |

## Mixing English Tenses (Rule-Based)

| Situation | Rule | Example |
|----------|------|--------|
| Future time clause | Use present, not will | When I finish, I will start |
| Future perfect | Action before future point | By the time she arrives, I will have finished |
| Cause (past → present) | Past explains present | I feel tired because I worked late |
| Since | Always past clause | I have lived here since I moved |
| Past before past | Use past perfect | I had finished before meeting |

## Conjunctions and Sentence Structure

### Coordinating Conjunctions (FANBOYS)

Example:

### Compound Sentence
Two independent clauses joined with a conjunction:
```

I like coffee, but I prefer tea.
```

## Adverb Clauses

### Structure
```

Subordinating conjunction + subject + verb

```

### Types
- Time: when, after
- Cause: because, since
- Condition: if, unless
- Contrast: although

### Rules
- Beginning → comma required  
- End → no comma  
- Middle → commas before and after  

---

## Common Errors

| Error | Correction |
|------|-----------|
| Because..., no comma | Add comma |
| Wrong conjunction | Use correct meaning |
| Fragment clause | Attach to main clause |
| Double conjunction | Remove extra |

---

## Reduced Adverb Clauses

### Key Rules
1. Same subject required  
2. Remove subject and conjunction  
3. Use V-ing or having + V3  

### Examples
- After she finished → After finishing  
- Because she was tired → Being tired  
- Because she had finished → Having finished  

---

## Noun Clauses

### Functions
- Subject: That he left surprised me  
- Object: I know that he left  
- Complement: My concern is whether he left  

### Tests
- Replace with "it"  
- Answer "what?"

## Final Technical Summary

1. Tenses are formed using verb structure or auxiliary combinations  
2. Irregular verbs must be memorized  
3. Stative verbs avoid progressive forms  
4. Clause relationships define tense interaction  
5. Reduced clauses improve clarity and conciseness
